  1. **The CRISPR Array:** This is a DNA sequence containing repeating segments (the “repeats”)

      • **Cas1 & Cas2:** Involved in the initial acquisition of spacer DNA from invading viruses.

          • **Cas5:**  A channel that facilitates DNA entry.                                      
          • **Cse1, Cse2, and Cas7:**  Other proteins involved in the process.                    
                                                                                                  
  3. **crRNA (CRISPR RNA):** A short RNA molecule that guides the Cas proteins to the target DNA. 
  The crRNA is derived from the spacer sequences within the CRISPR array.                         
  4. **PAM (Protospacer Adjacent Motif):** A short DNA sequence immediately adjacent to the target
  DNA sequence. The presence of a PAM is crucial for the Cas proteins to bind and cut the DNA.
